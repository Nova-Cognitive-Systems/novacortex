The 7-point answer-accuracy gap is one question where the fact was retrieved but the grader marked the (identical) answer down — an LLM/grader artifact, not a retrieval miss.

How we measured

Same questions, same model, two ways of supplying the project knowledge:

  • Dump — the entire knowledge base goes into the prompt every query (the "big CLAUDE.md / KB handover" pattern).
  • Retrieval — the KB lives in NovaCortex; only the top-5 relevant memories are retrieved per question.

We separate NovaCortex's job (retrieval) from the LLM's job (answering) with a recall@K metric: did we surface the gold facts each question needs? The handful of end-to-end misses are LLM/grader limits that hit the dump baseline equally, not retrieval failures. Fully reproducible: node scripts/benchmark/run.mjs and scripts/benchmark/scale.mjs.

And it's a privacy win, not just a cost one

Dumping the whole KB ships all of your knowledge to the model provider on every single query. Retrieval sends only the few relevant snippets. With NovaCortex self-hosted, the knowledge base never leaves your infrastructure; only the minimal retrieved context goes to the model.
